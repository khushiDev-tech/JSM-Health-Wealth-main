Commonly Asked Queries

ULIP (Unit Linked Insurance Plan) combine life insurance with market-linked investments. Premiums split between coverage and funds (equity, debt, or hybrid) based on risk. They offer flexible, long-term wealth creation and 100% tax-free maturity for goals like child's education or retirement.

  • Pay regular premiums (monthly/quarterly/yearly); portion funds life insurance, remainder invests in chosen funds.
  • Investment splits into units at fixed face value; unit value is Net Asset Value (NAV).
  • Grows with market performance; switch funds per conditions.
  • Receive fund value at maturity.
  • On death, family gets higher of Sum Assured + Total Fund Value (TATA pays remaining premiums per plan).

  • Insurance + Investment in One Plan
    ULIPs offer dual benefits: market-linked wealth creation and life insurance for you/family throughout the term.
  • Switch Between Funds
    Switch between equity/debt funds during term; allows 12 switches (per policy terms).
  • Premium Redirection
    Redirect future premiums to different funds anytime via policy number and allocation percentages.
  • Partial Withdrawals
    5-year lock-in; after, unlimited partial withdrawals from fund value without extra charges (per policy terms).

  • Myth: ULIPs Give Low Returns
    Reality: Modern ULIPs deliver competitive market-linked returns with much lower charges.
  • Myth: ULIPs Are Expensive
    Reality: IRDAI regulations have slashed charges, boosting cost-efficiency.
  • Myth: ULIPs Are High Risk
    Reality: Risk matches chosen fund (equity, balanced, or debt) per appetite.
  • Myth: ULIPs Are Complicated
    Reality: Simple to manage once fund allocation is understood; easy switching.
  • Myth: Market Fluctuations Affect Life Cover
    Reality: Life cover stays fixed; death benefit protected per terms.
